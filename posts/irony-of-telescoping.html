Irony of 🔭 ing

January 19, 2021

Let's say you are given a array of numbers and are asked to calculate: $$\max_{1 \le i, j \le n} a_i - a_j$$. What do you do?

Obviously this: $$\max_{i=1}^{n} a_i - \min_{i=1}^{n} a_i$$. Telescoping! We all love that.

But if you are asked to calculate the later one, what are the chances that the first expression would peep in your prepheral vision?

A Shortest Path Problem

You are given a weighted undirected connected graph consisting of $n$ ($2 \le n \le 2 \cdot 10^5$) vertices and $m$ ($1\le m \le 2 \cdot 10^5$) edges. No self-loops, no multi-edges. Let's define the weight of the path consisting of $k$ edges with indices $e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_k$ as $\sum_{i=1}^{k} w_{e_i} - \max_{i=1}^{k} w_{e_i} + \min_{i=1}^{k} w_{e_i}$, where $w_i$ — weight of the $i$-th edge in the graph. Find the minimum weight of a path from the $1$-st vertex to the $i$-th vertex for each $i$ $(2 \le i \le n)$.
