Ray Tracing Through Lenses: Convex Lens

biconvex-lens.ggb

$AOB$ is a convex lens. $u$ is the object and $v$ is the image. Drag $G$ to move and change the object. Drag $M$ up and down to visualize the span of rays from $G$ (head of the object) and their respective refracted rays (and refracted rays' span).

The two black rays are drawn to show the usual way to find the location of $G$'s image. And blue ray is to represent all the rays that come from $G$, pass through the lens and refracted afterwards.

If you put your eyes in the-

  • yellow shaded region, point $G$ will be visible to you, but will be blurry.
  • blue shaded region, you will see point $G$ as if it were at $G'$.
  • red shaded region, you won't be able to see $G$.
  • anywhere else (namely, white (color-less region)), you will see $G$ directly.

When you drag the object somewhere between left $f$ and $O$, the virtual image will be projected in this geogebra construction. But Shaded regions are no more :( (some objects become undefined, well, for obvious reasons). Perhaps the dependecies of the objects in geogebra can be done in such a way that everything remains defined.

But anyway, consider this as an exercise. Imagine yourself :)
